COCONUT SHRIMP WITH SPICY PEANUT DIPPING SAUCE

Description

In this gastronomic masterpiece, behold the harmonious dance of flavors and textures: the sweetness of crispy coconut shrimp, a tribute to the oceans' offerings, and the fiery embrace of a spicy peanut sauce, a condiment forged in the fires of culinary creativity. This sauce, a veritable elixir of boldness, lends its allure to every morsel it touches, whether shrimp, raw vegetables, grilled chicken, or even the humblest of root crops.

Shrimp Ingredients

  • 1/2 cup cornstarch
  • 1/4 teaspoon kosher salt
  • 1/4 teaspoon cayenne pepper, or to taste
  • 3 egg whites
  • 1 1/4 cups sweetened shredded coconut
  • 1/4 cup Japanese breadcrumbs (panko)
  • 1 pound extra-jumbo shrimp (16/20 count), peeled and deveined with tails left intact
  • Canola oil, for frying
  • Lime wedges, for garnish

Peanut Sauce Ingredients

  • 3/4 cup coconut milk
  • 2 (packed) tablespoons light brown sugar
  • 3/4 teaspoon tamarind concentrate (or paste)*
  • 1 tablespoon red curry paste
  • 2 teaspoons fish sauce
  • 2 1/2 tablespoons creamy peanut butter
  • 1/2 teaspoon paprika
  • 1/4 teaspoon minced garlic

Steps

  1. Mix cornstarch, salt, and pepper in a shallow dish.
  2. Whisk egg whites lightly in a second shallow dish.
  3. Combine coconut and panko in a third shallow dish, toss to mix evenly.
  4. Dredge a shrimp in cornstarch mixture, shake off excess.
  5. Dip shrimp in egg whites, ensuring full coating.
  6. Coat shrimp with coconut/panko mixture, press gently to adhere.
  7. Place coated shrimp on a clean plate.
  8. Repeat coating process for remaining shrimp.
  9. Cover plate, refrigerate for 10 minutes to 4 hours.
  10. Fill deep-fat fryer or deep saucepan with 3 inches of oil.
  11. Attach candy/deep-fry thermometer, heat oil to 365°F.
  12. Add coated shrimp to hot oil in batches.
  13. Fry shrimp until golden brown and crispy, about 2-3 minutes.
  14. Turn shrimp while frying for even browning.
  15. Transfer cooked shrimp to paper towel-lined cooling rack.
  16. Keep shrimp warm in a 200°F oven as you finish frying.
  17. Reheat peanut sauce if necessary.
  18. Arrange shrimp on serving platter.
  19. Garnish with lime wedges.
  20. Serve shrimp with warm peanut sauce for a delicious feast.
